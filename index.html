Base set
The base set of characters in either hiragana or katakana, also known as gojūon.
Diacritics
A diacritic mark is a mark added to a character (also called an accent mark). Diacritic marks indicate that the sound produced by that character is different than normal. Both hiragana and katakana contain two diacritic marks, both of which are added to the upper-right of characters: the dakuten, which resembles a quote mark, and the handakuten, a small circle. For an English example, consider the "é" in resumé.
Digraphs
Digraphs in the Japanese hiragana and katakana systems, also called yōon or youon, are pairs of kana that produce a different sound. Each pair consists of one gojūon character that ends in an "i" sound followed by a smaller version of the ya, yu, or yo kana. For example, in hiragana, combining き (ki) and や (ya) produces きゃ (kya). Examples of digraphs in English include "ch" and "th".
Hiragana
One of the two modern Japanese syllabaries (the other being katakana; the kanji writing system is not covered here). Hiragana is commonly used to write words for which there are no kanji (or for which the kanji are less well known), among other things.
Kana
The word kana refers to both the hiragana and katakana syllabaries and to the individual characters.
Katakana
One of the two modern Japanese syllabaries. Katakana is used for things like (but not limited to) writing foreign words, technical terms, or to add emphasis.
Syllabary
Because hiragana and katakana characters represent individual syllables, they are called syllabaries.
